Editorial Policy

How we score games, select reviewers, and protect our independence.

Independence

GameReview operates independently of any publisher, developer, or platform holder. We do not accept payment, gifts, or sponsored placement in exchange for reviews. No advertiser has any influence over our editorial scores or coverage decisions. Review copies received from developers are disclosed in the relevant review.

Our Scoring System

All games are scored on a scale of 1 to 10, where each number carries a specific meaning. We do not use decimals in editorial scores — a score is a considered judgment, not a calculation. The scale works as follows:

ScoreMeaning
10A landmark achievement. Essential playing for anyone who cares about games.
9Excellent. Minor flaws that do not detract from an outstanding experience.
8Great. A very good game with a few rough edges.
7Good. Enjoyable and worth your time, but falls short of its potential.
5–6Mixed. Has real strengths but notable weaknesses. For dedicated fans only.
1–4Poor to bad. Significant problems that undermine the experience.

Community Reviews

Community scores are calculated as a straight average of all submitted user ratings and are displayed separately from editorial scores. Community reviews are moderated for spam and abuse, but we do not edit or remove reviews based on opinion. Users are responsible for the accuracy of their own submissions.

Corrections

If a factual error is found in any review, we correct it promptly and note the correction at the bottom of the relevant page. Scores are not revised after publication except in exceptional circumstances, such as a game being fundamentally altered by a post-launch patch.
